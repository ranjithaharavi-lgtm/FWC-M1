\section*{Summary}

\begin{enumerate}
\item Coordinate Geometry is the study of geometry using algebra and coordinate systems.

\item The distance between two points $A(x_1,y_1)$ and $B(x_2,y_2)$ is given by
\[
AB = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}.
\]

\item Three points are said to be collinear if the distance between two points is equal to the sum of the distances between the other two points.

\item Using the distance formula, we can determine whether a triangle is isosceles or equilateral.

\item Two points are equidistant from a point if their distances from that point are equal.

\item The coordinates of any point on the x-axis are of the form $(x,0)$.

\item The coordinates of any point on the y-axis are of the form $(0,y)$.

\item The distance formula helps in identifying the type of quadrilateral formed by given points.

\item Coordinate Geometry has applications in navigation, engineering, and physics.
\end{enumerate}
